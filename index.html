  • Group Order Info

  • Group Orders, How do they work?

    When a group order is requested, I will ask the seller or fansite if they are open to an overseas orders and if there are any other group orders open for the US. If there is a group order already open, I will not open one. Please do not request I open a GO, when there is already a USA GO open.

    Once I get permission to open a GO, I will tweet the details of the item (cost, etc) and open a form. The form will close 1-2 days before the deadline just in case something goes wrong with the deposit. Once I close the form, I send the first payment to the fansite/seller. If there is a minimum to the GO and I dont hit it, I will refund everyone.

    Now is the waiting game. The seller or fansite will have the goods in production and when they are done, they will ship the goods to my korean address. Once the goods arrive at my korean address, I will send out an EMS estimate which I will split amoungst the buyers.

    As soon as every buyer has paid the EMS fee, I will send the payment to the korean address (warehouse) and the goods will be shipped to my house in Massachusetts. Once the goods arrive at my house, I will weigh and calculate the domestic shipping for each buyer and send out an email with the rate(s).

    Once the buyer pays the domestiv shipping fee, I will ship the goods!

  • Individual Orders

  • What are individual orders (I/Os)?

    Individual orders are orders where there are only 1 or possibly 2 buyers involved instead of multiple buyers.

  • How do I/Os work?

    Here is the process for I/Os:

    1: Using the I/O form, the order is placed. NOTE: Make sure to place the order WELL BEFORE the deadline; if the deadline is on the weekend in Korea, the latest you can submit an order for it is Thursday (EST) that same week. PLEASE BE AWARE OF TIME ZONES! S.Korea is 14h ahead of EST.

    2: I will review your order and confirm the details (availability, deadline). If there is an issue with the order (availability, deadline, or other), I will DM you. If the order looks good, I will send you a confirmation email tha includes the price of the order in USD and the korean domestic shipping (if applicable).

    3: Once I receive the money, I will deposit/pay for the item(s), then place the order (fill out the order form or place the order with the fansite directly).

    4: Once goods have shipped and arrive at my Korean address, I will wait until there are enough other goods so that EMS is cheap for you. This means your order might take longer (especially if I have to wait for more orders to reach my K-address). Please keep this in mind when you order. Please do not ask me for a time estimate because I can't give you one.

    5: Once I know EMS, I send you an email. After it's paid, I will have the items shipped to me.

    6: When items are at my house, I will send another email with a link to the domestic shipping form. After you fill it out, I will send an email with the USPS shipping rate. Once you pay your rate, I package and ship your item! (You must request, a different shipping method, if needed.)

  • How are I/Os different from GOs?

    I/Os are less formal than GOs. There is no form for each item, rather you use the general individual order form to place an order and the payment info will be sent in a confirmation email.

    Unlike GOs, the price for an item in an I/O will include the domestic shipping fee within Korea (i.e. the fee for the item to be delivered to my warehouse) and the bank transfer fee.

  • How long do I/Os take?

    This will depend on the goods ordered. If you ordered a doll, for example, it takes between 4 to 6 months. If you ordered a slogan, it can take between 1 to 2 months. Stickers and other small items can take up to a month.

    In addition to this, it can take up to a week or two after the I/O has arrived at my Korean warehouse for it to ship to my house because I have to wait until there are enough other goods so EMS/DHL is cheap for you.

  • Won't EMS fees be too high for I/Os?

    No, I will try to combine a lot of orders before shipping to my house; that way EMS/DHL will be cheaper.
